Giant Pandas

Giant Pandas once faced extinction due to habitat losses, but now Panda populations are increasing over the years! According to World Atlas, "Preservation endeavors have seen an increase in the number of panda reserves up to 67, which now safeguards almost 66% of wild pandas."

American Alligator

The American Alligator faced extinction back in the 1960s due to being hunted down. When their population was heavily decreasing, hunting became prohibited which then overtime resulted in a rise of their population. As of right now, the American Alligator is no longer endangered.

Humpback Whales

Humpback Whales once faced extinction due to mass hunts of its population. The Marine Mammal Protection Act protected Humpback Whales which helped their population increase over time. Currently, Humpback Whales are no longer endangered.

Eureka Valley Evening Primrose

The Eureka Valley evening primrose was once considered "A rare desert wildflower." This wildflower was endangered due to people off-roading. With the temporary closure of off-roading in Eureka dunes, the population of the Eureka Valley evening primrose has steadily increased.
